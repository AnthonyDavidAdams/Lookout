I'm writing to pitch a story for AARP The Magazine or AARP Bulletin about a real problem nearly every older adult faces: needing help with their computer and having no one available to ask.

We all know the phone call. A parent calls their adult child and says, "There's a box on my screen and I don't know what to do." The child asks, "What does it say?" The parent reads it wrong, or can't find it anymore, or gets frustrated. The child gets impatient. Everyone hangs up feeling bad.

I built a free tool called Lookout that eliminates this call entirely.

Lookout is an AI assistant that runs on any Mac. But unlike Siri or ChatGPT, it can actually see your screen. When you ask "How do I save this document?" it doesn't give a generic answer. It looks at exactly what's on your screen and says, "Click the blue Save button in the top-right corner of the window." Specific. Patient. No guessing.

It works like having a knowledgeable friend looking over your shoulder -- one who never gets annoyed, is available at 2 a.m., and never makes you feel silly for asking.

Here's what makes it different from other AI tools:

- It sees your actual screen, so guidance is specific to exactly what you're looking at
- It remembers your preferences and skill level across conversations
- It explains what it's doing and why, building confidence rather than dependence
- It's completely free and open source (CC BY-NC 4.0 license)
- It runs locally on your Mac -- screenshots are processed and immediately discarded, never stored on a server
- It installs with a single command and lives quietly in the menu bar as an eye icon

The only cost is a Claude API key from Anthropic, which runs about 1-3 cents per conversation. That's roughly a dollar a month for most people.

I'm offering this as a public utility through Earth Pilot (earthpilot.org), not as a commercial product. There's nothing to buy. I built it because I was tired of the phone calls too.

The GitHub page is here: https://github.com/AnthonyDavidAdams/Lookout
